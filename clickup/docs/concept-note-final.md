# Concept Note: AI Adoption and Transformation at TygrLabs (V4)

**Prepared by:** TYGR Ventures in partnership with Casper Academy and its L&D platform BrainWrk.ai

**Submitted to:** TygrLabs leadership
**Engagement duration:** 6 months

---

## 1. Why this matters now for TygrLabs

TygrLabs operates three connected service lines:
- B2B car booking platform for ride management
- Web development
- Creative and digital marketing services

Global experience shows a widening gap between organizations that adopt AI in a structured way and those that do not. AI leaders are achieving faster delivery cycles, higher quality, and better margins, while clients increasingly expect AI capable partners as a basic requirement.

Bangladesh is also moving in this direction, with export oriented IT and services companies beginning to embed AI into delivery. TygrLabs has an opportunity to position itself early within its peer group by building AI enabled ways of working across its B2B car booking, web development, and creative and digital marketing businesses.

This engagement is about making TygrLabs an AI ready company in practice, not in slogans.

---

## 2. The core problems we are solving

Across TygrLabs today, three connected problems limit performance and readiness for AI:

1. **Inconsistent and undocumented processes**
   Ways of working vary by individual and team. Many critical workflows are not fully documented, which makes it difficult to measure, improve, automate, or introduce AI safely.

2. **Fragmented tools and local optimizations**
   Different teams use different tools, reports, and workarounds without a shared architecture. Some local improvements exist, but they do not translate into organization wide capabilities.

3. **Skills and confidence gaps for AI enabled work**
   Team members are not yet supported with a clear path for learning how to work with AI and automation in their specific roles and service lines.

These three issues reinforce one another: weak processes resist automation, fragmented tools generate siloed data, and staff without support hesitate to adopt new AI enabled workflows.

---

## 3. Engagement overview and principles

TygrLabs will engage TYGR Ventures, Casper Academy, and BrainWrk.ai in a 6 month transformation program that treats AI as an enabler of better processes and capabilities, not a goal in itself.

The engagement rests on three principles:

- **Process before tools**
  Fix, standardize, and measure key workflows so they are ready for automation and AI.

- **Honest intervention classification**
  For each pain point, decide whether the right solution is process change, automation, AI augmentation, or skills development, instead of forcing AI where it does not fit.

- **Readiness gate**
  Use an explicit readiness assessment to decide where TygrLabs should and should not invest in AI. This protects the company from spending on AI before basic foundations are in place.

---

## 4. High level phase structure

At a high level, the engagement is organized into six phases:

1. **Phase 1: Discover and baseline (# weeks)**
   Map critical workflows in each service line, understand current tools and data, and baseline key metrics for speed, quality, and effort.

2. **Phase 2: Strategy and design (# weeks)**
   Apply the readiness gate, select the first set of high value pain points in each line of business, and design the 6 month roadmap with clear success metrics.

3. **Phase 3: Package and align (1 Week)**
   Align with leadership on priorities, governance, and roles. Confirm which teams will participate in the first impact cycles and how results will be reviewed.

4. **Phases 4 to 6: Sprint based impact cycles (2 - 8 weeks each cycle)**
   Instead of treating Phases 4, 5, and 6 as one long stretch, TygrLabs will run a sequence of short, focused impact cycles with individual teams. Each cycle is a compact version of execute, learn, measure, and plan next.

The first three phases set direction and guardrails. Phases 4 to 6 are where teams experience AI and automation in real delivery.

---

## 5. Sprint based cycles for Phases 4 to 6

Rather than only running one or two large pilots, TygrLabs will run **impact cycles** that focus on one team and one key pain point at a time. Each cycle:

- Targets a specific team, such as B2B car booking operations, a web development pod, or a creative and digital marketing squad.
- Focuses on one clearly defined pain point with measurable impact potential.
- Runs as a 2 to 8 week sprint, depending on complexity.
- Produces a live production pilot, transparent KPIs, and a clear decision on whether to scale, adapt, or retire the intervention.

A standard AI impact cycle has five stages:

1. **Select team and pain point (Cycle week 0 to 1)**
   Select one team and one concrete problem, such as manual ride dispatching, repetitive QA checks, or campaign reporting. Define baseline metrics, target improvements, and constraints for this cycle.

2. **Design solution and experiment (Cycle week 1 to 2)**
   Co design a focused intervention that may combine workflow changes, automation, and AI assistants. Configure a minimal but decision ready dashboard for the selected KPIs and agree a simple runbook for how the team will use the new workflow.

3. **Execute and learn in production (Cycle week 2 to 6)**
   Put the solution into real use with the team and real clients. Run short feedback loops, such as weekly check ins, to capture what is working, adjust prompts and workflows, and resolve friction points. Keep live KPI tracking visible to the team and leadership throughout.

4. **Scale or adapt (Cycle week 4 to 8)**
   If the pilot is successful, scale it within the same team and, where appropriate, adapt the pattern to a second service line. If results are mixed, capture learning and either iterate with a revised design or close the pilot consciously.

5. **Measure, document, and feed the roadmap (End of cycle)**
   Complete a focused ROI and maturity review for this team and pain point. Quantify impact against the agreed KPIs, reassess AI readiness in this area, and produce a short cycle report that feeds the 7 to 12 month roadmap and TygrLabs governance library.

Across the 6 month engagement, multiple cycles are sequenced so that TygrLabs builds a portfolio of validated pilots instead of isolated experiments.

## 6. Illustrative pilots for TygrLabs

Examples of impact cycles that fit TygrLabs context include:

1. **B2B car booking operations**
   - Automate or assist dispatching and ride scheduling for common scenarios.
   - Introduce AI supported customer communication templates for confirmations and updates.
   - Track response time, error rates, and ride utilization.

2. **Web development delivery**
   - Use AI assisted code review and test case generation on selected projects.
   - Standardize ticket triage and estimation flows so they can be automated over time.
   - Track cycle time, defect rates, and rework.

3. **Creative and digital marketing services**
   - Support campaign planning, content drafting, and performance reporting with AI tools inside a governed workflow.
   - Track content turnaround time, approval cycles, and campaign performance against baseline.

These examples are indicative. The exact pilots will be chosen during Phases 1 to 3 using TygrLabs data and leadership priorities.

---

## 7. Expected outcomes by month 6

By the end of the 6 month engagement, TygrLabs should expect:

- **Two or more production pilots that have passed the readiness gate and impact review** in priority areas across the three service lines.
- **Documented and improved workflows** for selected teams, with clear process maps and ownership.
- **Live KPI dashboards** for the chosen pilots and for key parts of the delivery system, operated by TygrLabs staff.
- **A data backed roadmap for months 7 to 12** that shows which pilots to scale, which areas to strengthen before further AI use, and which new opportunities to explore.
- **A more confident and capable workforce**, with targeted training delivered through Casper Academy and BrainWrk.ai tied to real workflow changes.
